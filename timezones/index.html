The simplest way to schedule across time zones

Coordinating a meeting when people are spread across cities and countries is genuinely hard — but it shouldn't require a spreadsheet or mental arithmetic. This tool keeps it simple: tell it who's meeting and where they are, and it shows you the best time to meet, written out in each person's own local time. No UTC, no "is that my time or yours?"

How it works

Add each person and pick their city (you can search by city or country — "Milan", "Vietnam", "Tokyo" all work). Set the hours everyone's generally available, and the tool finds the time that lands inside the most people's day. If a time works for everyone, great — you'll see it front and center. If your team is spread too far for a perfect overlap, it shows you the next-best options and how many people each one suits.

It handles daylight saving automatically

Time zones shift with daylight saving on different dates around the world. This tool uses your device's official time zone data, so the times it shows are correct for today — no manual adjusting.

Built for remote teams and global friends

Whether you're scheduling a standup across three continents, a call with an overseas client, or a game night with friends abroad, the goal is the same: find a time that works, fast, without anyone doing time-zone math in their head.

Frequently asked questions

How do I use it?
Add each person and their city, set the hours everyone's roughly free, and read the answer at the top — it's shown in everyone's local time. That's it.
What if no time works for everyone?
It automatically shows the best available time and how many people it suits, plus alternative times you can tap to compare.
Can I search by country?
Yes — type a city or a country ("Milan", "Germany", "Vietnam", "Tokyo") and it finds the right zone.
Does it handle daylight saving?
Yes, automatically, using your device's official time zone database.
Is my data private?
Everything runs in your browser; nothing is sent to a server. See our Privacy Policy.
