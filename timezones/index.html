The easiest way to schedule across time zones

If you work on a remote or distributed team, you already know the pain: someone's in London, someone's in San Francisco, someone's in Singapore, and finding an hour that works for all three feels impossible. This free timezone overlap finder does the math for you. Enter each teammate's city and their normal working hours, and it instantly highlights the windows where everyone is awake and on the clock at the same moment — no mental arithmetic, no spreadsheets, no "wait, is that my time or yours?"

How the overlap finder works

Behind the scenes, the tool converts everyone's local working hours to a shared universal timeline (UTC), then looks for the hours where all your people overlap. When a perfect overlap exists, you'll see the best meeting window front and center. When no single hour works for everyone — common with very spread-out teams — it falls back to finding the slot that works for the most people, so you can still get the largest group together and follow up with the rest.

Daylight saving is handled for you

One of the trickiest parts of cross-timezone scheduling is daylight saving time, which starts and ends on different dates in different countries. Because this tool uses your browser's built-in time zone database, it accounts for DST automatically for the date you're planning around — so a meeting that looks good today won't secretly break when the clocks change.

Also includes a fast time zone converter

Need to quickly answer "what time is 3pm New York in Tokyo?" — switch to the Converter tab. Pick a time and a starting zone, add as many destination zones as you like, and see them all at once, with clear labels when a time lands on the previous or next day. It's perfect for scheduling a call, catching a live event, or coordinating a handoff between regions.

Tips for scheduling across time zones

  • Aim for the edges of the workday. Early morning for one region is often late afternoon for another — those edges are where overlaps usually hide.
  • Rotate the pain. If one teammate always takes the late call, switch it up so the inconvenience is shared fairly.
  • Record what you can. When a true overlap is impossible, record the meeting so people in the off-hours can catch up asynchronously.
  • Share the link. Use the "Share this setup" button to send your team the exact configuration so everyone sees the same windows.

Frequently asked questions

Is this timezone finder free?
Yes, completely free with no sign-up. Add as many people and zones as you need.
Does it handle daylight saving time?
Yes. It uses your browser's official time zone database, which knows each region's DST rules, so the times shown are correct for the date you're planning around.
What if no time works for everyone?
The tool automatically finds the slot that works for the most people, and shows how many are available, so you can still gather the largest possible group.
Can I save or share my team setup?
Yes. Your setup is remembered in your browser for next time, and the "Share this setup" button creates a link you can send to teammates so they see the same overlap.
Is my data private?
Everything runs in your browser. Your team list isn't sent to or stored on any server. See our Privacy Policy for details.
